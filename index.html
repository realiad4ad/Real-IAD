Real-IAD: A Real-World Multi-View Dataset for Benchmarking Versatile Industrial Anomaly Detection

Abstract

Industrial anomaly detection (IAD) has garnered significant attention and experienced rapid development. However, the recent development of IAD approach has encountered certain difficulties due to dataset limitations. On the one hand, most of the state-of-the-art methods have achieved saturation (over 99\% in AUROC) on mainstream datasets such as MVTec, and the differences of methods cannot be well distinguished, leading to a significant gap between public datasets and actual application scenarios. On the other hand, the research on various new practical anomaly detection settings is limited by the scale of the dataset, posing a risk of overfitting in evaluation results. Therefore, we propose a large-scale, Real-world, and multi-view Industrial Anomaly Detection dataset, named Real-IAD, which contains 150K high-resolution images of 30 different objects, an order of magnitude larger than existing datasets. It has a larger range of defect area and ratio proportions, making it more challenging than previous datasets. To make the dataset closer to real application scenarios, we adopted a multi-view shooting method and proposed sample-level evaluation metrics. In addition, beyond the general unsupervised anomaly detection setting, we propose a new setting for Fully Unsupervised Industrial Anomaly Detection (FUIAD) based on the observation that the yield rate in industrial production is usually greater than 60\%, which has more practical application value. Finally, we report the results of popular IAD methods on the Real-IAD dataset, providing a highly challenging benchmark to promote the development of the IAD field. The dataset and evaluation code will be open-sourced in the future.

Motivation

Due to limitations in the dataset, the development of Industrial Anomaly Detection (IAD) methods has encountered certain difficulties in recent years. On one hand, most state-of-the-art methods have already reached saturation (AUROC exceeding 99%) on mainstream datasets such as MVTec-AD, making it challenging to differentiate between methods and resulting in a significant gap between public datasets and real-world application scenarios (More challenging datasets are needed to support further research in the AD field). On the other hand, research on various new practical anomaly detection settings is constrained by the dataset size, which poses a risk of overfitting evaluation results (Larger-scale datasets are needed). Furthermore, in industrial production, the yield rate typically ranges from 60% to 100%, which contradicts the existing experimental settings with 100% yield rate (Settings that are more aligned with practical application value are required).

Contribution

  • This paper introduces a new dataset, named Real-IAD, which is over ten times larger than existing mainstream datasets. It comprises 30 categories of objects, each captured from 5 different angles, totaling 150K high-resolution images. Real-IAD features a larger defect area and a wider range of defect ratios, enabling a more effective differentiation of the performance of various methods, and catering to a variety of research settings in IAD.
  • Furthermore, a Fully Unsupervised Industry Anomaly Detection (FUIAD) setup, which is closer to real-world application scenarios, has been constructed based on the Real-IAD. This setup only uses the naturally occurring constraints where the yield rate of most production lines is greater than 60%, without introducing additional manual annotations.
  • We report the performance of common IAD methods on the Real-IAD dataset under several settings, and provide a highly challenging benchmark to promote the development of the anomaly detection field.
